Tomatillo Chicken & Corn Skillet

Gather the Ingredients Below

Ingredient Amount
Chopped Chicken Breast 10 ounces
Corn 2 ears
Sweet Peppers 4 ounces
Gaucamole 1/4 Cup
Fromage Blamc 2 Tablespoons
Tomatillo - Poblano Sauce 1/3 cup
Cannellini Beans 1 7.75
Lime 1
Garlic 2 Cloves
Sliced Pickles Jalpeno Pepper 1 ounce
Grated Cotija Cheese 2 Tablespoons
Chicken Demi-Glace 2 Tablespoons
Instructions for Preperation:
  1. Prepare the Ingredients:

    Wash and dry the fresh produce

    Remove any husks and silks from the corn; cut the kernels off the cobs.

    Peel and roughly chop 2 cloves of garlic. Cut off and discard the stems of the sweet peppers; remove the cores, then thinly slice into rings. Drain and rinse the beans. Combine in a bowl.

    Quarter the lime.

    Roughly chop the Jalapeno pepper. throughly wash your hands immediately after handling

  2. Cook the chicken:

    Pat the chicken dry with paper towels; season with salt and pepper.

    In a medium pan(cast iron or non-stick, if you have one) heat 1 teaspoon of olive oil on medium-high until hot

    Add the seasoned chicken in an even layer. Cook, without stirring, 3 to 4 minutes, or until lightly browned.

    Continue to cook, stirring occassionally, 3 to 4 minutes, or until browned and cooked through.

    Leaving any browned bits(or fond) in the pan, transfer to plate

  3. Cook the vegetables:

    In the pan of reserved fond, heat 1 teaspoon of olive oil on medium high until hot.

    Add the corn kernels in an even layer. Cook, without stiring 2 to 3 minutes, or until lightly browned (be careful, as the corn may pop as it cooks).

    Add the prepared garlic, sweet peppers, and beans; season with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring ocasionally, 3 to 4 minutes, or until slightly softened.

    Add the tomatillo-poblano sauce (carefully, as the liquid may splatter), demi-glace and 1/4 cup of water. Cook, stirring occassionally, 2 to 3 minutes, or until thickended and throughly combined. Turn off the heat.

  4. Make the creamy gaucamole:

    While the vegetables, in a bowl, combine the gaucamole,fromage blanc, and the juices of 2 lime wedges. Taste, then season with salt and pepper if desired.

  5. Finish & serve your dish:

    to the pan of cooked vegetables, add the cooked chicken and as much of the chopped jalapeno pepper as you'd like, depending on how spicy you'd like the dish to be. Stir to thoroughly combine. Taste, then season with salt and pepper if desired.

    Serve the finished chicken and vegetables topped with the creamy guacamole and cheese. Serve the remaining lime wedges on the side. Enjoy!
